This year has been challenging...

Especially for teachers

I used this opportunity to briefly interview two of the most passionate and hardworking teachers I know, and ask them what this year has been like for them. From adjusting to life in a pandemic to adjusting to virtual and hybrid classrooms, it hasn't been easy...
But one thing's for sure: It has been a learning experience!

What has been your biggest challenge this year?

Mrs.Hines: Definitely figuring out how to instruct my class using mostly technology. At the beginning of the school year we were 100% virtual, and it was a bit of a challenge figuring out the technology.

Ms. Colbert: Effectively teaching virtual learners and getting them to form meaningful connections to the content I was teaching was difficult. Many of my students are low-income and didnâ€™t have much experience with technology. They are also first graders who are used to hands-on learning. I had to come up with ways of teaching that were engaging and held their interest, all while helping them to become more familiar with technology and how to navigate it.

What was your biggest success in the classroom?

Mrs.Hines: Finally understanding the virtual classroom was a huge win for me! Once I did that, things went a bit more smoothly. There are still some problems, but not nearly as many as before.

Ms. Colbert: Helping my students learn how to navigate their technology and software was a huge success. Once they understood how to do that, virtual and hybrid learning was a much smoother process. Also, getting the kids to keep their masks on!

What did you learn?

Mrs.Hines: I must say that I learned that empathy is quite important. I always like for parents to have empathy and understanding towards all that goes into successfully teaching their children. However, this year I had to have empathy for parents struggling with virtual learning. Things can be difficult for parents juggling multiple children and multiple tasks.

Ms. Colbert: I learned that children are very capable of learning and understanding technology quickly! Even my five and six year olds are pretty "tech-savvy" after months of practice!

Crystal Colbert 2021
